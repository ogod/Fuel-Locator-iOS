Thanks for using Fuel Locator!

This app is designed to intuitively display petrol prices around Western Australia. It displays a variety of fuel types and brands for country and metropolitan regions using a map and graph interface. The data is preprocessed to make it as accurate as possible and present statistical information as reliable as possible, and so is capable of presenting more data more accurately than any other similar app on the market today.

Map interface

Fuel stations are marked on the map by annotations bearing the logo of the station brand, the current price including discounts if activated (see settings) and the exact discount in brackets if applicable. The annotations are color coded and prioritized according to the fuel price on the day for the selected fuel type. This means that if the display is cluttered, more expensive stations are eliminated to make the cheaper prices clearer. If two stations have a similar price rank, then they are consolidated with the number of stations displayed in the annotation glyph to make the locations of the best prices as unambiguous as possible, but still enable the user to understand the choices available.

There are ten price ranks, each representing 10% intervals of the prices for the region. The green ranks represent the cheapest stations, while the salmon colored ranks represent the highest prices. The yellowish ranks represent the median prices.

Fuel prices are presented for particular day, fuel product and region. To select a different day or fuel type, tap on the label at the top of the map. The region is controlled by the most common region of the displayed stations on the visible map area. If most of the stations displayed are in the southern metropolitan area, then that will be the region drawn on for statistics, otherwise if enough come from the wider metropolitan area, than that will be used instead.

Discounts are approximated for each brand and default to be 4c per liter. The discounts applied can be controlled in the Fuel Locator settings pane in the Settings app.

Highly detailed information is presented in a callout box with a tap of the finger on any station annotation. Information presented includes the fuel price, station name, its address, phone number, the brand discount if activated, and special features, such as opening hours, driveway service and so on. If you tap on the callout, the app will take you to the map and show you how to get there from wherever you are.

History

The history view allows you to view fuel price changes over the previous several years so that you can gain a balanced side of how much you should reasonably be paying for fuel now. The statistics will vary depending on the date and fuel type plus the region that you are currently viewing on the map. A violet line also indicates the 7-day running mean of those fuel prices.

The colors used are the same as on the map; the lowest prices are green and the highest are salmon colored.

In the graph shown, the thickest regions represent the lowest price density. Each color band contains the same number of prices, so the thinnest bands represent the greatest price density. Thus the yellowish bands usually appear the thinnest as they represent the prices of the majority of suppliers.

The graph can be zoomed horizontally and moved around by using standard screen gestures.

Split View

On the iPad the Fuel Locator app allows split view. This allows the user to look at the map or any other app at the same time as Fuel Locator. In particular this is useful if the user wants to look at routes to different stations without having to constantly switch apps.
